email tokens

(02)

Figma kit for diverse stakeholders, streamlining usage across various departments and improving user experience in mail communications.
Year
2023
Copyright
Handshake

Objective
Handshake already had a great marketing strategy and a brand built by the team's hard work. My goal was to create a token system and a set of templates for email communication.
Problem Statement
In the era of social media and information noise, a mailbox is like a safety island on the road. A place where you get the information you really want. There's still room for long texts and you can build trusting relationships. That's why adapting the brand's style and communication principles within an email is not just about trying to fit all the content into a 600-pixel width. Email chains and their style should be consistent.
Challange
I had two weeks to complete this task. The design system was still in development at the time. I had to create something that would bring the company's colourful and varied letters together into one confident system.
Approach
I did a bit of research into the best ways to do email communication and email newsletters. Then I gave Handshake a modern kit and token system in Figma. This system was easy for every department to understand and use, and it made sure that Handshake's users were always communicating in the same way.

To meet the challenge of completing a large amount of work within tight deadlines, I utilized an organized and efficient design process by finding or reworking key components and creating various layouts so I can make design system scalable.
Solution
I've chosen to use the Atomic Design Methodology. The client has different departments that use different tools and communicate in different ways, so I have to make sure that I deliver the components at the conceptual level.
Output
I've created five user journeys in two categories (Employer Journey and Student Journey) and a number of templates for the client.

1. The employer journey has two separate onboarding flows: one for the job lifecycle and one for the career fair lifecycle.
2. The student journey has four weekly phases and 12 templates for various purposes: gifts, news, helpful articles, employer offers, etc.ii. Token system with components, modules and templates.
3. Components that match the general style guide.
4. Up to 100 modules that scale well for email layouts.
5. Master templates for longreads, subscription confirmation, promo landings, surveys, events, blog posts, etc.
My role
Consistency keeper
The consistency keeper's job is to make the brand new face stand out in customers' mailboxes.
Process
  1. Research. I've looked at 35 different brand email chains, making sure I have enough time to analyse them properly. This will help me define what's good and what's bad in terms of their email marketing.
  2. Design system adaptation. I've separated all the elements of the existing design system and found the right place for them.
  3. Layout design. I've made sure that the hierarchy, typography and colours are guided well.
